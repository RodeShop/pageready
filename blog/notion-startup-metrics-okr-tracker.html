Why Startups Need a Metrics System

You raised your seed round three months ago. Your investors want monthly updates. Your team needs quarterly OKRs. And you're supposed to know your MRR, DAU, CAC, LTV, and runway off the top of your head.

The reality? Most founders track their metrics across five different tools: Stripe for revenue, Google Analytics for users, a spreadsheet for burn rate, a Google Doc for OKRs, and their memory for everything else. When investor meetings come up, they spend an hour pulling numbers from different tabs.

This isn't a tools problem. It's a systems problem. You need one place where every number connects to every other number — where your MRR feeds into your OKR progress, where your weekly check-in shows trends over time, where your runway calculation updates automatically as your burn rate changes.

What's Inside

Metrics Database

Your KPIs in one place. Track MRR, DAU, CAC, LTV, runway, NPS — any number that matters to your startup. Each metric has a Current Value, Target Value, Unit, and Tracking Frequency. The Linked OKRs relation shows you which objectives each metric supports.

OKRs Database

Your quarterly objectives. Set 3-5 per quarter, assign owners, and track confidence levels. The Status field shows On Track, At Risk, Behind, Completed, or Cancelled. The KR Count rollup tells you how many key results each objective has.

Key Results Database

The measurable outcomes that prove you achieved your objectives. Each KR has a Start Value, Current Value, and Target Value — and the Progress % formula auto-calculates how far you've come. Link each KR to an Objective and watch the progress roll up automatically.

Weekly Check-ins Database

Your Monday morning ritual. Record MRR, DAU, burn rate, and runway every week. Add highlights, blockers, and next week priorities. Over time, this becomes your startup's heartbeat — the place you go to see if you're improving or slipping.

How It All Connects

The magic is in the connections. When you update a Key Result's Current Value, the Progress % recalculates. When you add a Metric and link it to an OKR, you can see which objectives depend on which numbers. When you complete a Weekly Check-in, you have a historical record of how your key numbers have changed over time.

This isn't a template with four separate databases. It's one system where every number tells a story.
