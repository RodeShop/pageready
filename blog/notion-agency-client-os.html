Why Small Agencies Need a Client System

You have 6 clients. Each has 2-3 projects. You're tracking everything in spreadsheets, email threads, and your memory. Last week you forgot to send an invoice. The week before, you missed a deadline.

This is how most small agencies manage their work: scattered across tools that don't talk to each other. Your project tracker doesn't know about your invoices. Your invoice tracker doesn't know about your proposals. And your memory? It's terrible at remembering which client needs what.

The problem isn't that you don't care about your clients — it's that the tools we use for agency management are fundamentally disconnected. Your projects live in one app. Your invoices live in another. Your proposals live in your email. And your client list? It's a spreadsheet that nobody updates.

Introducing the Agency Client OS

The Agency Client OS is a Notion template built for small agencies who need to manage clients, projects, tasks, proposals, and invoices without the overhead of dedicated agency software. It connects five databases — Clients, Projects, Tasks, Proposals, and Invoices — so you can see the full picture of your agency in one place.

Every project links to a client. Every task links to a project. Every invoice links to a client. When you open the template, you don't just see a list — you see the story of your agency.

What's Inside

Clients Database

Your agency's client list. Track status, industry, contact info, and monthly retainer. The Total Revenue rollup shows lifetime value per client — so you know who your most valuable relationships are.

Projects Database

Every deliverable for every client. Track deadline, budget, hours logged, and hourly rate. The Task Count rollup shows how many tasks each project has — so you can see progress at a glance.

Tasks Database

The individual steps that make up each project. Track status, priority, and due date. Link each task to a project to see all your work for one deliverable.

Proposals Database

Your sales pipeline — from pitch to acceptance. Track status (Draft → Sent → Accepted), amount, and response time. Follow up on Sent proposals before they go cold.

Invoices Database

Your billing pipeline — from draft to paid. Track status (Draft → Sent → Paid → Overdue), amount, and payment dates. The Overdue filter shows who owes you money.

How It All Connects

The magic is in the connections. When you add a project and link it to a client, you can see all your work for that client. When you add an invoice and link it to a client, the Total Revenue rollup updates automatically. When you add a proposal and link it to a client, you can track your sales pipeline.

This isn't a template with five separate databases. It's one system where every piece of your agency tells a story.

FAQ

Q: Can I add more project types?

A: Yes. Common additions: Video Production, Photography, Copywriting, SEO, Paid Ads.

Q: How do I track team members?

A: Add a "Team Member" select property to Projects and Tasks to assign work.

Q: Can I use this for freelancing, not agencies?

A: Yes. The system works for solo freelancers too — just ignore the team-related features.
