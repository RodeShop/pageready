Why Job Seekers Need a Notion System

You've applied to 20 jobs this month. Your email has 47 unread "application received" messages. You have a Google Sheet that hasn't been updated in two weeks. And that follow-up email you meant to send to the recruiter at Stripe? It's still sitting in your drafts, three days past the deadline.

This is what happens when you job search without a system. Not because you're disorganized — but because the tools we use for job searching are fundamentally broken. Email is for communication, not tracking. Spreadsheets are for data, not relationships. And your memory? It's terrible at remembering which company you applied to on LinkedIn vs. which one came through a referral.

The solution isn't "be more organized." It's having a connected system where every piece of your job search talks to every other piece.

Introducing the Job Search Command Center

The Job Search Command Center is a Notion template built specifically for people who are applying to multiple jobs at once. It connects four databases — Companies, Applications, Interviews, and Contacts — so you can see the full picture of your job search without switching between tabs, apps, or sticky notes.

Every application is linked to a company. Every interview is linked to an application. Every contact is linked to a company. When you open the template, you don't just see a list — you see relationships.

What's Inside

Companies Database

Track every company you're targeting. Set their status (Interested, Applied, Interviewing, Offer, Rejected, Ghosted), add their industry, website, and Glassdoor rating. The Total Applications rollup shows you exactly how many roles you've applied to at each company — no manual counting needed.

Applications Database

Every role you've applied for goes here. Add the position, salary range, date applied, and a follow-up date. The Days Since Applied formula automatically calculates how long you've been waiting — so you know exactly when it's time to send that check-in email. The Interview Count rollup tells you how far you've progressed without counting rounds manually.

Interviews Database

Schedule every round — phone screens, technical interviews, portfolio reviews, culture fits, final rounds. Add the interviewer's name, prep notes, and what outcome you're hoping for. When you open the calendar view, you see your entire interview schedule at a glance.

Contacts Database

Recruiters, referrals, hiring managers, peers. Track their email, LinkedIn, and when you last reached out. This is your networking CRM — the people who can move your application forward.

How It All Connects

The magic of this template is the connections. When you add an application and link it to a company, that company's Total Applications count updates automatically. When you schedule an interview and link it to an application, the Interview Count rolls up. When you add a contact and link them to a company, you know exactly who to reach out to when you need a referral.

This isn't a template with four separate databases. It's one system where everything talks to everything else.

FAQ

Can I use this if I'm only applying to a few jobs?

Yes. The template works whether you're applying to 3 roles or 30. Start small and scale up as your search grows.

What if I'm not in tech?

The template works for any industry. The sample data shows tech companies, but the database structure works for healthcare, finance, marketing, education — any field.

Can I track multiple job searches separately?

Yes. Add a "Search" select property to Applications if you want to separate full-time roles from contract work, or job searches in different cities.
